Key Notes on Table

  • Pipeline Infrastructure: Most projects leverage existing pipeline infrastructure rather than building new major interstate pipelines; lateral connections (short connecting pipelines from main lines) are common with faster permitting as intrastate
  • Geographic Concentration: Projects co-located with production (Chevron Permian, New Era Helium Permian, Oracle Stargate near West Texas gas) avoid long-distance pipeline needs; Haynesville Shale (Louisiana) and Permian Basin (West Texas) provide abundant, proximate supply
  • Technology Preferences: Mix of aeroderivative and industrial gas turbines; simple-cycle turbines for faster deployment vs. combined-cycle for efficiency; typical sizes 2.5 MW to 430 MW per unit; mobile/modular units from VoltaGrid, Solaris Energy Infrastructure for rapid deployment
  • Timeline Drivers: Grid queue times now 5-10 years in major data center markets; behind-the-meter gas operational in 12-24 months; permits streamlining under current federal policies; direct pipeline laterals avoid grid interconnection delays
  • Environmental Features: 5 projects include CCS provisions (45Q tax credit eligible); hybrid approaches combining gas with solar, wind, and battery storage; some designed for future hydrogen compatibility; advanced emissions controls (SCR systems, DLE technology)
  • Contract Structures: Typically 5-10 year gas supply agreements with extension options; fixed-price gas where possible; behind-the-meter configurations to avoid utility rate structures and grid costs
  • Market Scale: Data centers could consume 6-10 Bcf/day of natural gas by 2027 (~10% of total US consumption); more than 25% of new facilities >500 MW will have BTM power by 2030 (up from 1% today); over 200 gas-fired power units in development for data center sector
  • CCS Technology Leaders: ExxonMobil leads with >90% capture using proprietary technology and 900+ mile Gulf Coast CO₂ pipeline network; GE Vernova's EGR systems can reduce CCS costs by ~6% while enabling 95%+ capture; industry utilizing Shell CANSOLV, Svante, BASF, and Linde technologies
