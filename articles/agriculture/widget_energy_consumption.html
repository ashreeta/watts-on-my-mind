Data Sources and Methodology

% Direct Energy (Function Categories): Primary source is USDA/DOE "U.S. Agriculture and Forestry Greenhouse Gas Inventory: 1990-2013" providing fuel-type breakdown (Diesel: 44% | Electricity: 24% | Natural Gas: 13% | Gasoline: 11% | Propane/LPG: 7%). These fuel percentages were mapped to functional categories based on predominant uses documented in agricultural energy literature.

Functional Allocations:

  • Field Operations (44%): All diesel consumption - tractors, combines, harvesters are primary diesel users
  • Irrigation Pumping (13%): Proportionally allocated from 24% electricity total based on USDA NASS Farm and Ranch Irrigation Survey
  • Building Heating (13%): Mapped to natural gas/propane consumption
  • Light Equipment (11%): All gasoline consumption (pickups, ATVs, UTVs)
  • Crop Processing/Drying (8%): Majority of propane/LPG plus small electricity portion
  • Ventilation/Cooling (7%): Remaining electricity allocation
  • Material Handling (4%): Residual propane use plus electric forklifts

Current Electrification Rates: Source-specific data from University of Georgia Extension (irrigation 80% electric), Iowa State University Extension (crop drying 20% electric), manufacturer specifications (tractors <1% electric), and industry surveys (building heating <10% electric heat pumps, material handling ~30% electric forklifts).

Feasibility Ratings: Based on technology readiness level (TRL), economic competitiveness, and infrastructure requirements synthesized from equipment manufacturers, ACEEE Agricultural Energy Efficiency Opportunities, university extension cost-benefit analyses, and US DOE data.
