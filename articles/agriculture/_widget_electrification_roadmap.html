Agricultural Electrification Roadmap

Technology Readiness and Timeline (2025-2040) • On-Farm Equipment Electrification
ROADMAP SCOPE: On-Farm Equipment Only (126-168 TWh of 450 TWh Non-Electric Agricultural Energy)
✓ INCLUDED IN THIS ROADMAP
On-Farm Equipment (126-168 TWh, 28-37%):
  • Tractors & field equipment (diesel displacement)
  • Irrigation pumps (diesel to electric/solar)
  • Building heating & cooling systems
  • Grain dryers (propane to electric)
  • Light vehicles: pickups, ATVs, UTVs (gasoline displacement)
✗ NOT INCLUDED
Other Agricultural Energy (280-324 TWh, 62-72%):
  • Fertilizer/pesticide manufacturing (180-220 TWh): Indirect, off-farm chemical production requiring natural gas
  • Food processing & distribution (100-120 TWh): Beyond farm gate operations
Note: Total U.S. agricultural sector energy consumption is ~550 TWh, of which ~100 TWh is already electric. This roadmap addresses electrification potential for specific on-farm equipment categories within the remaining 450 TWh of fossil fuel use. The majority of agricultural energy consumption occurs in off-farm fertilizer production and post-harvest food processing/distribution, which are outside the scope of equipment electrification strategies.
